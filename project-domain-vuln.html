Domain Vulnerability Remediation

Systematic identification and remediation of security vulnerabilities across Clarios' global domain infrastructure, working with cross-functional teams to improve web application security, SSL certificate management, and overall cybersecurity posture.

Project Overview

Clarios operates domains across global operations, each supporting critical business functions from marketing sites to customer portals. However, many of these domains had accumulated security vulnerabilities over time, creating potential risks for customer trust, regulatory compliance, and overall security posture.

I was tasked with identifying and remediating these vulnerabilities, working directly with domain owners across different regions and departments to implement security fixes and improve Clarios' BitSight security score.

Key Focus Areas

Web Application Headers

Missing or misconfigured security headers that left applications vulnerable to common web attacks.

Web Application Security

Various security misconfigurations and outdated protocols affecting site integrity and user safety.

SSL Certificate Management

Expired or weak SSL certificates compromising encrypted communications and customer trust.

The Process

01

Vulnerability Assessment

Used BitSight security monitoring platform to analyze reports and identify vulnerabilities across Clarios' domain portfolio. Focused on high-impact issues affecting company security, prioritizing based on severity and potential business impact. Created a comprehensive inventory of vulnerable domains requiring remediation.

02

Domain Matching & Analysis

Developed Excel formulas to efficiently cross-reference vulnerable domains identified by BitSight against our internal domain ownership list. This streamlined the process of identifying actionable domains with established contacts and likely owners, making the remediation process much more efficient than manual lookup.

03

Cross-Functional Communication

Contacted domain owners across different global teams with specific vulnerability details tailored to their technical context. Documented ownership and maintained clear communication channels to ensure accountability throughout the remediation process.

04

Remediation Guidance

Provided domain owners with specific, actionable remediation guidance and technical documentation. Explained the security implications in business terms, making it clear why fixes were necessary and how they would protect customer data and improve regulatory compliance.

05

Progress Tracking & Follow-up

Tracked remediation progress systematically across all affected domains. Followed up on implementation to ensure fixes were properly applied and verified through BitSight that vulnerabilities were successfully resolved. Maintained detailed records for compliance and future reference.

Technical Challenges

Legacy Infrastructure

Many vulnerable domains were legacy sites with unclear ownership or outdated technology stacks. Required detective work to identify current maintainers and assess whether sites should be updated or decommissioned.

Global Coordination

Working across different time zones and departments required clear, asynchronous communication and well-documented guidance that teams could follow independently.

Prioritization Strategy

Developed a systematic approach to prioritize vulnerabilities based on severity, business criticality, and remediation complexity, ensuring high-impact issues were addressed first.

Key Learnings

  • Security Requires Collaboration: Technical vulnerabilities can't be fixed in isolation. Success required building relationships with domain owners, understanding their constraints, and providing practical support.
  • Clear Communication is Critical: Translating security vulnerabilities into business impact helped stakeholders understand urgency and prioritize fixes appropriately.
  • Data-Driven Efficiency: Using Excel to cross-reference domains saved significant time compared to manual lookup, demonstrating how simple automation can multiply effectiveness.
  • Infrastructure Hygiene: Many vulnerabilities stemmed from legacy sites that should have been decommissioned. Regular infrastructure audits prevent security debt accumulation.
  • Real-World Security: Gained practical experience with security monitoring tools, SSL/TLS management, and how theoretical vulnerabilities manifest in production environments.
