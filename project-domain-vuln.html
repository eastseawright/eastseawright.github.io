Domain Vulnerability Remediation

Found and fixed security issues across Clarios' global websites by coordinating with domain owners in different regions, improving the company's security rating and protecting customer data.

Role UX/UI Applications Intern
Company Clarios
BitSight Security Vulnerability Assessment Excel Analysis SSL/TLS Management Cross-functional Collaboration

Project Overview

Clarios has websites across the globe supporting everything from marketing to customer portals. Over time, many of these sites had developed security issues — expired certificates, missing security settings, outdated configurations — that put customer trust and compliance at risk.

I was responsible for identifying these issues, tracking down the right people to fix them, and following up until the fixes were verified. The result was a measurably improved company security score.

Key Focus Areas

Missing Security Headers

Many sites were missing basic security settings that protect against common web attacks.

Outdated Configurations

Security misconfigurations and old protocols were leaving sites exposed to known threats.

Expired SSL Certificates

Some sites had expired or weak security certificates, breaking encrypted connections and eroding customer trust.

The Process

01

Identify the Problems

Used BitSight, a security monitoring platform, to scan Clarios' domains and identify vulnerabilities. Prioritized issues by severity and business impact, creating a clear list of what needed to be fixed first.

02

Match Domains to Owners

Built Excel formulas to cross-reference the vulnerable domains with our internal ownership records. This turned a manual lookup process into something that could be done in minutes instead of hours.

03

Coordinate Across Teams

Reached out to domain owners across different departments and regions with clear descriptions of each issue. Kept records of who was responsible for what so nothing fell through the cracks.

04

Provide Fix Instructions

Gave domain owners specific, step-by-step guidance for each fix. Explained the business risk in plain terms so they understood why it mattered and could prioritize accordingly.

05

Track and Verify

Tracked progress across all affected domains, followed up on outstanding items, and confirmed through BitSight that each fix was applied correctly. Kept detailed records for compliance documentation.

Technical Challenges

Old Sites, Unknown Owners

Many vulnerable domains were legacy sites that nobody clearly owned anymore. Required detective work to find the right contacts and decide whether to fix or shut down each site.

Working Across Time Zones

With domain owners spread across the globe, I had to write clear, self-contained instructions that teams could follow on their own schedule without needing a meeting.

Deciding What to Fix First

Built a system to rank vulnerabilities by how severe they were, how important the affected site was to the business, and how hard the fix would be — so the most critical issues got addressed first.

Key Learnings

  • Security Is a Team Sport: Fixing vulnerabilities requires building relationships with domain owners, understanding their priorities, and giving them practical support.
  • Speak the Business Language: Explaining security issues in terms of business risk helped stakeholders understand urgency and act faster.
  • Simple Automation Saves Hours: Using Excel formulas to cross-reference domain lists saved significant time compared to manual lookups.
  • Clean Up Old Sites: Many issues came from legacy sites that should have been shut down. Regular cleanup prevents security problems from piling up.
  • Hands-On Security Experience: Gained practical experience with security monitoring tools, SSL certificate management, and how security vulnerabilities play out in real production environments.
