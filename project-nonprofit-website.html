UWM Safety & Assurances Website Redesign

Complete website redesign and content migration for the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee's Safety and Assurances department, improving user experience and consolidating critical safety resources into a unified platform.

Role Web Development Intern
Timeline Aug 2022 - May 2023
Organization UWM Nonprof-IT
WordPress Sitefinity CMS UX/UI Design Agile Methodology Content Strategy

Project Overview

As part of the UWM Nonprof-IT program, our team worked with the University's Safety and Assurances department to completely redesign their web presence. The existing site had fragmented information across multiple platforms, outdated design, and poor user experience that made it difficult for students, faculty, and staff to find critical safety resources.

Our goal was to create a modern, accessible website that consolidated all safety-related information into one intuitive platform while migrating from the legacy web identity system to UWM's new standard.

Challenges

Content Fragmentation

Safety information was scattered across old systems, off-site pages, and outdated documents that needed to be consolidated.

Navigation Complexity

The existing navigation structure made it difficult for users to find emergency contacts and safety protocols quickly.

Platform Migration

Moving from Web Identity 1.0 to 2.0 while maintaining content integrity and improving the user experience.

Stakeholder Alignment

Coordinating with multiple departments to ensure the new site met diverse organizational needs.

Approach & Methodology

01

Discovery & Research

Conducted stakeholder interviews with the Safety and Assurances team to understand their goals, audience needs, and pain points. Analyzed user behavior on the existing site and identified the most critical information that needed prominent placement.

02

Information Architecture

Redesigned the site structure and navigation menu to prioritize emergency resources and frequently accessed information. Created a clear hierarchy that matched mental models of how users search for safety information.

03

Content Migration & Optimization

Migrated content from the legacy platform to UWM's Web Identity 2.0 system. Consolidated off-site content and restructured pages for better readability and accessibility. Updated outdated information and improved content quality.

04

User-Centered Design

Designed intuitive layouts with clear visual hierarchy. Made emergency contacts and critical safety procedures immediately accessible from the homepage. Ensured mobile responsiveness for access during emergencies.

05

Agile Collaboration

Worked in sprints using Agile methodology with regular check-ins with our client. Maintained transparent communication throughout each phase, incorporating feedback iteratively. Our team collaborated effectively to meet deadlines and deliver quality work.

Key Features Delivered

Optimized Homepage

Redesigned the homepage to immediately surface the department's value proposition and primary services. Clear calls-to-action for emergency contacts and safety resources.

Streamlined Navigation

Created an intuitive primary navigation menu that reduced clicks to important information. Implemented breadcrumbs and clear page hierarchies for better wayfinding.

Comprehensive Documentation

Provided detailed documentation for the client team, including content management guides, maintenance procedures, and best practices for future updates.

Accessibility Compliance

Ensured the site met WCAG accessibility standards with proper heading structure, alt text for images, and keyboard navigation support.
