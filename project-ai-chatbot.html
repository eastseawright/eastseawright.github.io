AI Battery Assistant Chatbot

An intelligent conversational AI system built with Microsoft Copilot Studio that helps customers instantly find battery specifications and product information, informed by user journey analytics to optimize the knowledge base.

Role UX/UI Applications Intern
Timeline 2024
Company Clarios
Microsoft Copilot Studio Large Language Models Hotjar Analytics Conversational AI

Overview

Customers visiting Clarios' Optima battery website struggled to find warranty information and battery specifications. The original user journey was 5+ minutes long with excessive clicks, often ending at a generic contact page without answers. This friction impacted customer confidence, ease of access, and ultimately sales.

I developed an AI-powered chatbot using Microsoft Copilot Studio that reduced the warranty identification journey from 5 minutes to 20 seconds, providing instant, specific warranty information based on users' exact needs.

The Problem

Lengthy User Journey

Finding warranty information required 5+ minutes of navigation through multiple pages, resulting in user frustration and drop-off.

Poor Accessibility

Critical warranty details were buried deep in documentation, making it difficult for customers to verify coverage before purchase.

Lost Sales Opportunities

Customers unable to quickly confirm warranty details often abandoned purchases or contacted competitors with more accessible information.

Solution Approach

01

Warranty Information Prioritization

Focused the chatbot's primary purpose on making battery and charger warranties easily accessible. The bot helps users find exact warranty information for specific products and use cases, prioritizing this critical information that directly impacts purchase decisions and customer confidence.

02

Natural Language Processing

Built using Microsoft Copilot Studio to ensure user-friendly, conversational interaction. Customers can ask questions in plain English like "What's the warranty on the Optima RedTop?" and get instant, specific answers rather than generic contact information.

03

Data-Driven Knowledge Base

Uploaded warranty manuals and user guides to the bot's knowledge base. Used Google Analytics to identify popular searches and prioritize topics based on common customer queries, ensuring the bot could answer the questions customers actually asked most frequently.

04

Brand-Aligned Interface

Designed a simple, intuitive Optima Bot icon that aligns with Clarios' brand identity. The interface is clean and accessible, making it easy for customers to engage with the bot immediately upon visiting the site.

Technical Implementation

Conversational Design

Created dialogue flows that feel natural and helpful, not robotic. Designed prompts that guide users while allowing flexibility in how they phrase questions.

Knowledge Base Architecture

Structured product data and FAQs in a way optimized for LLM retrieval. Implemented metadata tagging and semantic relationships to improve answer relevance.

Analytics Integration

Connected Hotjar session recordings and heatmaps to identify where users struggled in their journey. This qualitative data drove knowledge base prioritization decisions.

Specific Warranty Answers

The chatbot transformed the customer experience by reducing warranty identification time from 5+ minutes with excessive clicks to just 20 seconds with instant, specific answers. This improvement directly enhanced customer confidence, increased sales conversion, and provided better ease of access to critical product information. The bot outputs warranty information tailored to users' specific needs rather than directing them to generic contact pages.

Key Learnings

  • User Research is Critical: Hotjar analytics revealed customer needs that weren't obvious from product data alone. Real user behavior should drive AI training priorities.
  • Conversation Design Complexity: Creating natural dialogue flows requires anticipating many edge cases and gracefully handling ambiguous queries.
  • Continuous Improvement: LLM-based chatbots improve over time with more data. Built systems to monitor unsuccessful queries and expand the knowledge base accordingly.
  • Trust Through Transparency: Users appreciated when the bot acknowledged limitations and offered human handoff rather than providing uncertain answers.
