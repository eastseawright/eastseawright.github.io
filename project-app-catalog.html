Enterprise Application Development

Application Catalog

A searchable directory for all of Clarios' business applications, built with Microsoft Power Apps. Employees can quickly find the right tool for their job, see who owns it, and request access — all in one place.

Role UX/UI Applications Intern
Company Clarios
Microsoft Power Apps SharePoint Power Automate Enterprise Architecture

Overview

Clarios had no central place to view the 900+ applications used across 7 global regions. Multiple apps were doing the same thing, and nobody had a clear picture of who owned what or where it was available. Teams wasted time searching for the right tool or accidentally building something that already existed.

I designed and built an Application Catalog using Microsoft Power Apps that serves as the single source for all business applications. It sits at the front of the Global Application SharePoint site so anyone in the company can find what they need.

The Challenge

Scattered Information

Application data lived in different spreadsheets and systems, so nobody had a complete picture of what tools were available.

Different Needs by Role

Different teams and regions needed to see and filter applications differently based on their specific responsibilities.

Growing Scale

The solution needed to handle hundreds of applications today and keep working as the company adds more across new regions.

Solution & Implementation

I built a Power Apps application connected to SharePoint as the data source. Here's what it does:

01

One Place for Everything

All applications are organized by what they do. Employees and contractors can browse or search to find the right app for their needs, instead of guessing or asking around.

02

Flexible Search & Filtering

Users can filter by business function, region, owner, or keyword. The filters work together so you can narrow down results quickly — for example, finding all finance apps available in Europe.

03

Detailed App Profiles

Each application has its own page showing a description, what it's used for, where it's available (regional or global), and who the IT and business owners are. No more guessing who to contact about an app.

04

Direct Access & Requests

From the search results, users can launch an app directly, request access through the company's ticketing system, or view more details. This cuts out the back-and-forth of figuring out how to get started with a tool.

Technical Highlights

Performance with Large Datasets

Wrote optimized formulas to keep search and filtering fast even with hundreds of records, working around Power Apps' data limits.

SharePoint Backend

Set up a clean data structure in SharePoint with proper relationships between lists, making the data easy to maintain and expand.

Clean, Consistent Design

Designed an intuitive interface that works on desktop, tablet, and mobile so employees can access the catalog from anywhere.

Impact & Results

The Application Catalog is now a go-to tool for IT governance at Clarios. It saves employees time finding the right applications, helps leadership make better decisions about software investments, and makes it easier for global teams to collaborate.
